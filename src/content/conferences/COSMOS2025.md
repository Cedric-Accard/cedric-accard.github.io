**Speaker:** Cédric Accard  
**Collaborators:** Matthieu Béthermin; ALPINE, CRISTAL & COSMOS (Web) collaborations  
**Venue:** COSMOS conference  
**Date:** 20 May 2025

## Motivation

- The Kennicutt–Schmidt (KS) relation links gas and star formation rate (SFR) surface densities and is well studied at \(z < 4\), but remains poorly constrained at higher redshift.
- ALPINE–CRISTAL provides the first resolved [CII] observations of galaxies at \(z \sim 5\), enabling sub–kpc tests of the KS relation in the early Universe.

## Data and Methods

- HST and JWST resolve unobscured UV–to–NIR emission; ALMA resolves obscured SFR and gas via [CII] at kpc scales.
- Resolved SFR maps are obtained via SED modeling with CIGALE, after homogenizing all bands to ALMA’s resolution.
- Variability and uncertainties are quantified by generating mock images with correlated noise and repeating the full CIGALE pipeline \(\times 100\), yielding log–normal SFR errors and normal [CII] errors.
- The relation \(\ln \Sigma_{\mathrm{SFR}} = \ln C + a \ln \Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}} \pm \sigma_i\) is fitted using maximum–likelihood estimation, allowing tests of noise resampling and source bootstrapping.

## Key Results

- A tight \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}}–\Sigma_{\mathrm{SFR}}\) relation is found with low intrinsic scatter (\(\sim 0.15\) dex) and a **sub–linear slope** for the population (\(a \approx 0.9\)).
- Individual galaxies show **steeper, super–linear slopes** (\(a \approx 1.3\)), highlighting a difference between within–galaxy and population–averaged trends.
- Results broadly agree with Béthermin et al. (2023), but the shallower population slope suggests sensitivity to sample selection and fitting methodology.

## Extension to KS and Conversion Factors

- Translating [CII] luminosity to gas mass relies on conversion factors \(\alpha_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\); classical calibrations (e.g. Zanella et al. 2018) and revised simulation–based factors (Vallini et al. 2025) can significantly alter physical interpretations.
- Using high–redshift simulation frameworks (SERRA, NewHorizon, NewCluster) indicates a **mass–dependent trend**: massive (\(M_\star \gtrsim 10^{10} M_\odot\)) ALPINE–CRISTAL galaxies drift toward a starburst–like KS regime and shorter depletion times.

## Takeaway Messages

- The resolved SFR–[CII] relation at \(z \sim 5\) shows:
  - Sub–linear slopes for the population versus super–linear slopes within galaxies.
  - Very low scatter, possibly influenced by sample selection and methodology.
- Physical conclusions are highly sensitive to the adopted [CII]–to–gas conversion, motivating deeper work with simulations to derive physically grounded relations.
- There is evidence for a steepening KS slope and starburst–like depletion times in very massive galaxies at early cosmic times—underscoring the need to be careful about **what** is fitted and **how** it is fitted.
