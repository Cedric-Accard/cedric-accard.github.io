**Venue:** Tokyo meeting (high‑z galaxies)  

## Context and Motivation

- At \(z > 2\), star formation (SF) drives galaxy growth and shapes star‑formation histories, but the Kennicutt–Schmidt (KS) relation \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{SFR}}–\Sigma_{\mathrm{gas}}\) is still poorly constrained at high redshift.
- CO becomes challenging to observe at high \(z\); alternative gas tracers are needed.
- [CII] 158 µm is a bright ISM coolant that correlates with SFR and gas, yet is multi‑phase and its high‑z behaviour is complex.

## Observational Setup

- HST and JWST provide sub‑kpc resolved UV–NIR maps of unobscured SF.
- ALMA resolves obscured SF and traces molecular gas at kpc scales via [CII] 158 µm.
- The ALPINE–CRISTAL sample: 13 galaxies at \(4 < z < 6\) with \(10^{9.5} < M_\star < 10^{10.6} M_\odot\), observed at high resolution.
- Resolved SFR maps are built via SED modeling with CIGALE, combining these multi‑wavelength data.

## Resolved \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}}–\Sigma_{\mathrm{SFR}}\) Relation

- A tight resolved [CII]–SFR relation is found with:
  - Sub‑linear slope \(\beta = 0.87\).
  - Intrinsic scatter \(\sigma_i \approx 0.19\) dex over \(\log \Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}} \approx 6.8–8.6\) \(L_\odot \,\mathrm{kpc}^{-2}\).
- Compared to Herrera‑Camus et al. (2015) for nearby galaxies:
  - Slope is shallower but compatible (\(\beta = 1.13\)).
  - Scatter is similar (\(\sigma_i \approx 0.21\) dex), but the Tokyo work extends into a higher \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\) regime.
- Within individual galaxies the relation is steeper (\(\beta \sim 1.3\)), consistent with [CII] saturation behaviour in Ferrara et al. models.

## Extending to the KS Relation: [CII]–Gas Conversion

- **Zanella et al. (2018):**  
  - Use a constant \(\alpha_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\) conversion calibrated with CO for main‑sequence galaxies up to \(z \le 2\).
  - Places the sample along lines of constant depletion time, extending the locus of lower‑z discs—suggesting a gas‑rich but non‑exotic picture.
- **Vallini et al. (2025):**  
  - Provide a revised, \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\)‑dependent conversion factor from SERRA simulations for \(M_\star > 10^{9} M_\odot\) galaxies at \(4 \le z \le 9\).
  - Bridge disk and starburst regimes; many galaxies re‑classified with **starburst‑like depletion times** despite main‑sequence labels.
  - Similar trends appear in simulations such as NewCluster and THESAN‑ZOOM.

## Takeaway Messages

- [CII] is a powerful tracer of SF at \(z \sim 5\), yielding:
  - A tight resolved relation with \(\beta \approx 0.87\) and \(\sigma_i \approx 0.2\) dex.
  - Steeper slopes within galaxies at high \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\), but globally shallow population trends.
- The KS relation at \(z \sim 5\) is **conversion‑limited**:
  - At \(z < 2\), a constant \(\alpha_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\) implies depletion times \(t_{\mathrm{dep}} \sim 0.5–1\) Gyr and a gas‑rich main‑sequence picture.
  - At high \(z\), \(\Sigma\)‑dependent \(W_{\mathrm{[CII]}}\) yields slopes \(\beta \approx 1.8\) and \(t_{\mathrm{dep}} \sim 0.1\) Gyr, suggesting starburst‑like regimes.
- Simulations (SERRA, NewCluster, THESAN‑ZOOM) support short‑\(t_{\mathrm{dep}}\) and steep KS slopes in massive high‑z galaxies.
- Outlook:
  - Physically motivated, simulation‑anchored [CII]–gas conversions are essential; until then, gas masses and depletion times at \(z \gtrsim 4\) remain uncertain by at least a factor of \(\sim 5\).
